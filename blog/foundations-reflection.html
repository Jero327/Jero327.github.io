Foundations Reflection

26 September 2023

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

Identity & Values: Through core learning, I've gained a deeper understanding of my identity and values. I've realized that my identity is not fixed but rather a dynamic concept that can evolve over time. I've also discovered that my values are not set in stone, and they can change as I gain new experiences and insights. This has allowed me to be more open-minded and adaptable, as I'm more aware of the fluid nature of my identity and values.

Emotional Intelligence: Core learning has helped me develop greater emotional intelligence. I've become more attuned to my own emotions and those of others. I've learned to better manage my reactions and responses in various situations, leading to improved relationships and communication. This increased emotional intelligence has allowed me to navigate challenging circumstances with more empathy and grace.

Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset: Core learning has also introduced me to the concept of neuroplasticity and the power of a growth mindset. I've come to understand that my brain is capable of adapting and growing throughout my life. This knowledge has encouraged me to embrace challenges and setbacks as opportunities for growth rather than as failures. It has led to a more resilient and optimistic outlook, as I now believe in my ability to learn and improve continuously.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values guide ethical decision-making and the quality of code, empathy enhances user-centered design and collaboration, and self-awareness supports continuous learning and career development in the programming field. These factors are not only important for technical proficiency but also for the ethical and human-centered aspects of programming.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

The most surprising aspects of core learning are its profound significance, highlighting the unexpected impact of soft skills like emotional intelligence and self-confidence on a software developer's career. Additionally, core learning leads to self-discovery and the optimization of inner qualities. Initially, one might underestimate its importance, but it goes beyond technical skills. Soft skills, often overlooked in the software development field, play a pivotal role in communication, collaboration, and emotional management. Moreover, core learning unveils hidden facets of one's personality, fostering self-awareness , and enabling the effective utilization of innate strengths. These surprises underscore the holistic nature of personal and professional growth in software development.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The most challenging aspects of core learning revolved around the process of creating a structured learning plan. In the field of software development, the sheer breadth and depth of knowledge can be overwhelming. Determining how much ground to cover and how deeply to delve into each topic is a daunting task. Quantifying the scope of learning is elusive.

Furthermore, the abundance of learning resources adds complexity. Selecting the most relevant and effective resources among a vast array can be challenging. Balancing time allocation for each area of study and aligning it with end goals becomes a formidable challenge. In essence, the most challenging aspect of core learning lies in charting a clear and effective learning path amidst the vast and dynamic landscape of software development education.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Emphasizing core learning in a web development Bootcamp course is pivotal. It establishes a robust foundation, fostering lifelong learning, adaptability, and problem-solving skills. This approach cultivates professionalism, ethical coding, and effective collaboration. It prepares students to embrace industry trends, ensuring they can pivot to new technologies. Quality takes precedence over speed, resulting in clean, efficient code and enhanced employability. Furthermore, it boosts confidence, autonomy, and error prevention capabilities. Ultimately, core learning equips students with the essential knowledge and skills required for success in the ever-evolving field of web development, enhancing their employability and professional growth.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

Studying core learning is not a waste of time; it's a valuable investment. While programming practice is crucial, core learning provides a foundation for sustainable growth. It imparts fundamental principles, problem-solving skills, and ethical considerations essential for long-term career development. Emphasizing quality over quantity, it instills coding standards and professionalism. Core learning also fosters adaptability to evolving technologies, a critical skill in the fast-paced tech industry. Moreover, it encompasses soft skills like communication and teamwork, vital for real-world projects and collaboration.
