2. elegantly solved a problem

  • what was the problem?

    • When working with JavaScript and DOM manipulation of HTML elements, I encountered a problem. There was a button with an event listener attached to it, designed to add a 'li' element to a container every time it was clicked. Additionally, each 'li' element had its own event listener. The issue arose when testing the event listeners on the 'li' elements, as they were not functioning as expected.

  • what problem-solving techniques did you use?

    • To address this challenge, I employed various problem-solving techniques. I started by conducting online searches to gather insights and solutions. I also used the 'Rubber ducky method,' a technique where I explained the problem step by step as if teaching it to someone else, which often helped me identify the root cause. However, the most effective technique I utilized was 'console.log.' Placing 'console.log' statements at different points within the code allowed me to trace the execution sequence and pinpoint the exact location where unexpected behavior occurred. This significantly expedited the troubleshooting process.

  • how did you feel throughout the process?

    • Throughout this problem-solving journey, I experienced a mix of frustration and curiosity. Frustration emerged when I couldn't immediately identify the issue, but curiosity motivated me to explore different approaches and learn from the debugging process.

  • what did you learn?

    • Ultimately, I learned a valuable lesson: when attaching event listeners to dynamically created elements like 'li' elements, it's crucial to place the listener within a parent event handler, rather than outside of it. This ensures that the event listener is properly bound and executed, leading to the desired functionality. This experience not only improved my debugging skills but also reinforced the importance of efficient problem-solving techniques in programming."
